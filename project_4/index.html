The Rise And Fall Of Unisex Names

Is Eden a name for girl or for a boy? And what about Maé, Andrea, Tenzin? And are there names that change sides over time?

By Dominik Balmer

Published July 31, 2019

Swiss parents don't like unisex names for their babies. In the past 20 years there were only 21 unisex names - from an overall of the top 1000 baby names each year.

Names like Camille, Eden, Lou and Lilian are predominantely used for girls. Names like Elia, Eli, Enea, Lorin, Noe, Quinn, Robin are predominantely used for boys.

Sasha made a development from a predominantely male name to a female one. The other way went the actually mostly male names Anik and Eden - they used to be in the fast predominantely female names.

Andrea: The Special One

Elis and Maé took an interesting tour. Both started as mostly female names, then turned - but in the last few years they came back to the female side of the charts.

Only a few names are more or less stable - and stuck around the 50 percent. So do Charlie, Noa and Tenzin. But there is one name that is on top of alle the others - according to a fair distribution: Andrea. The explanation is simple: the name is used in the Italian part of Switzerland as a male name - and as a female name in the German part.
