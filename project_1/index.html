In Rape, Swiss Judges Are Lenient

An evaluation of 76 rape sentences shows that the judges seldom go high above the minimum punishment

By Dominik Balmer

Published July 12, 2019

The Judges at the Swiss local Supreme Court in the Canton of Zürich (German: Obergericht des Kantons Zürich) don't treat rape crimes harsh. The minimum custodial sentence for rape according to the Swiss Crimial Code is 12 Months - the range goes up to 120 Months.

An Analysis of all the 76 rape cases from 2014 till now shows that the Judges mostly give a custodial sentence just above the minimum. Almost all the punishments are in the lower third of the punishment range. Swiss legal experts demand higher custodial sentences for rape.

In some rare cases the Judges can because of mitigating circumstances go below the minimum. This could be the reason for some punishments below the minimum custodial sentence.
